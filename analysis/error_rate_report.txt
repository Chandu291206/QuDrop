QU DROP - ERROR RATE ANALYSIS REPORT

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------
This report analyzes the performance of the Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) process across different protocols (BB84, B92, E91, Six-State) under simulated channel noise. Specifically, a constant bit-flip noise of 5% (p=0.05) was injected into the quantum channel to measure its impact on the Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER) and subsequent file encryption integrity.

Notice that without Information Reconciliation (Error Correction) implemented, any QBER > 0 causes Alice and Bob to have differing keys post-Privacy Amplification, leading to high (~50%) Bit Error Rates during the classical file transfer phase due to the avalanche effect of the hashing.

CONCLUSIONS
--------------------
1. QBER closely tracks the injected channel noise (5%) for all protocols.
2. Due to the lack of Information Reconciliation in the simulation, even a 5% channel noise results in mismatched sifted keys. Privacy amplification (SHA-256) magnifies these errors, causing the final transferred files to have a ~50% Bit Error Rate and ~100% Byte Error Rate.
3. This proves the absolute necessity of implementing the Error Reconciliation phase (e.g., Cascade or LDPC) in a complete QKD pipeline to achieve a 0.00% BER in file transfer.
